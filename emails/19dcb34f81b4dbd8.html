The Empire Unmasked: Elite Impunity and the Slow Collapse of American Legitimacy

From: George Noble Apr 26, 2026 Label: Investing AI Summary 
The essay argues American power remains largely intact but legitimacy is failing rapidly via three mechanisms: (1) Accountability theater — the Epstein saga as a Rosetta Stone: a sprawling elite network narrowed to one dead man and one accomplice; the bar for elite prosecution requires 'perfect evidence, not persuasive pattern,' creating a permanent epistemic trap where what observers can infer far exceeds what the legal system acknowledges. (2) Market manipulation — clusters of uncannily well-timed trades before presidential policy announcements are visible in aggregate but individually defensible as 'aggressive speculation'; what everyone can see cannot be prosecuted. (3) Dollar hegemony erosion — analysts now openly discuss shift to currency multipolarity as states hedge against US governance risk; the 'sell America' trade is emerging in discourse in a way it didn't a decade ago. Internationally: the 'liberal order' narrative has been hollowed; the 'hypocrisy charge' (proclaiming universal norms while violating them) is now mainstream even in establishment foreign policy fora. The Trumpian 'war on Iran' has flipped the geopolitical chessboard of the Middle East in ways that cannot be reassembled. Author's conclusion: not a sudden collapse but a drawn-out imperial unraveling — hollowing of institutions, oscillation between oligarchy and populist catharsis, fragmentation of the global order into competing spheres.
