Mistral AI's €105M Memo, Enterprise SaaS Defense, Seed Math Broke

From: The VC Corner Apr 26, 2026 Label: Investing AI Summary 
Key insights: (1) Enterprise SaaS defense reset — AI bundling and rapid model improvements collapsed standalone pricing assumptions in under a year; survival now requires distribution control, product depth, and tight sales-usage alignment. (2) Seed math broke — entry prices have compressed upside so severely that wide portfolio diversification no longer generates fund-level returns; funds must size into fewer, higher-conviction positions. (3) Mistral's €105M seed memo: raised at 4 weeks/6 employees/no customers by framing open distribution as a control layer strategy (not goodwill), aligning investors around competitive positioning before any technical execution was visible. (4) AI deployment gap is the real bottleneck — large enterprises get hands-on implementation support while smaller teams self-serve and convert poorly; structured onboarding drives conversion far more than better models. (5) GPT-5.5 vs Claude Opus 4.7: performance gains real but uneven across applied benchmarks; teams prioritize reliability in workflows over leaderboard positions. Notable new funds: Sideline Group $155M (sports/media/entertainment), Mighty Capital $91M Fund III, Newfund Heka €60M (European early-stage). Hot deals: Vertical Bridge $1.5B from KKR (telecom infrastructure), Blue Energy $380M, Wasabi Technologies $250M (cloud storage), Reliable Robotics $160M (autonomous aviation), Pudu Robotics $150M at $1.5B.
