The business of being watched...



April 26, 2026



EDITOR'S NOTE: Today's newsletter covers the business of reality TV — the state of the genre, behind-the-scenes drama, and what Brew writers are watching.



THE TRIBE HAS SPOKEN — Is reality TV washed?

The number of new unscripted shows in the US fell 15% last year, now a third below its 2022 level. HGTV released episodes of only 35 shows in 2025 vs. 78 in 2019. Food Network produced half as many shows as 2019. Streaming platforms ordered 31% fewer reality series in 2025 than the prior year.



ECONOMIC REALITY: Industry consolidation, the end of the streaming wars, and declining ratings of iconic brands like The Bachelor and Real Housewives are driving cuts. But Love Island USA was the most-watched original streaming show last year (18B minutes streamed on Peacock).



EVERYTHING IS REALITY TV NOW: Cable now covers ~50% of US households (down from ~90% in 2010). YouTube accounts for ~13% of US viewing time. MrBeast has 430M+ subscribers. Twitch has 21M+ active streamers. Celebrity/athlete streams are up 20% YoY.



BEHIND THE SCENES DRAMA:

- The Bachelorette: Season postponed indefinitely 3 days before March premiere after video surfaced of star Taylor Frankie Paul

- Love is Blind Season 10: Manosphere accusations, sexual assault lawsuit, allegations of producer editing

- America's Next Top Model: New Netflix documentary brings fresh criticism of Tyra Banks for lack of accountability

- Real Housewives of NYC: Lawsuit by former star against Bravo and Andy Cohen proceeding in federal court



LONGEST RUNNING: Survivor just premiered its 50th season (debuted 26 years ago), renewed for 2 more. Cops (1989) is the oldest reality show; removed from Paramount in 2020, picked up by Fox Nation 2021.
