Last Week Ignite - 4.26.2026

From: Ignite Insights Apr 26, 2026 Label: Ignites AI Summary 
Five themes from April 21-26: (1) End of independent AI labs: Amazon committed ~$25B to Anthropic (100B+ AWS spend over 10 years, 5GW compute); Google committed $40B at $350B valuation (another 5GW on TPUs). SpaceX took a $60B option on Cursor with $10B upfront. Three of four major independent AI assets are now attached to hyperscalers. The path to a new standalone AI lab is now much narrower — compute economics require a hyperscaler partner. (2) Cursor's deal confirms that even the fastest-growing AI software companies can't fund compute through traditional venture rounds. Founders building thin product layers on frontier models: you need a workflow, regulated dataset, distribution channel, or compliance perimeter that the underlying model can't replicate. (3) OpenAI shipped GPT-5.5 six weeks after GPT-5.4 (82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0, up from 75%). Frontier AI now updates faster than enterprise procurement cycles. (4) Google TPU 8t/8i chips signed OpenAI as a customer for some workloads — the first crack in Nvidia's monopoly. Multi-substrate AI infrastructure software (compilers, schedulers, orchestration layers for Nvidia/TPU/Trainium routing) is now investable. (5) X-Energy IPO: priced at $23 (vs. $16-19 range), 15x oversubscribed, opened +31%, implied market cap $12B+. Nuclear/SMRs now viable because hyperscalers (projecting $700B+ combined capex) are the customer base. Watch geothermal, advanced grid software, and 24-hour baseload power startups. (6) China's NDRC instructed top AI startups (Moonshot AI, StepFun, ByteDance) to reject American capital without government approval. Cross-border venture is now formally bifurcated in both directions. The Cohere-Aleph Alpha merger positions as a European/Canadian sovereign alternative. Fed meeting April 28-29 adds macro risk: if Warsh comes in hawkish, cost of capital for AI infrastructure financing rises and the IPO window tightens.
