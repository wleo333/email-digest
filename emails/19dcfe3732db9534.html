The Only Startup Growth Guide You'll Need in 2026

From: The VC Corner Apr 27, 2026 Label: Investing AI Summary 
Core argument: most startups don't have a growth problem, they have a math problem. Key frameworks: (1) Cohort analysis is the only real PMF test — if your newest cohort retains worse than the oldest at the same age, you're losing ground; (2) Retention math: 8% monthly churn = ~1 year average customer life; if CAC payback takes 15 months, you lose money on every signup; (3) 'Fully loaded CAC' includes all salaries, commissions, tools, agency fees, and founder time — not just ad spend; many founders are 'buying users' without knowing it; (4) Retention plateau matters: a line that stops falling and stays flat means the product has become part of users' routines; a line falling toward zero means people find it interesting but don't truly need it; (5) Growth hacking in 2026 is improving the first 5 minutes of product experience, not viral tricks — algorithms now require real engagement; (6) Master one channel to profitability before touching a second; (7) 5 key diagnostic questions: What happens if you stop paid marketing tomorrow? Are recent cohorts improving vs. earlier ones? Does retention stabilize at a meaningful level? Are you solving an urgent vs. merely convenient problem? Does growth come from users discovering value on their own or from your team pushing harder each month?
