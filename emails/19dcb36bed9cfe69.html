From: George Noble Apr 26, 2026 Label: Investing AI Summary 
Panel with Peter Boockvar, Jack (Nobody Special), Geoff Garbacz, and Ross Hendricks. Key takeaways: (1) AI trade is splintered — semis exhibiting '1999-type behavior' while software falls through the floor; if hyperscaler earnings (this week: Meta, Google, Amazon, Microsoft, Apple) show 'just maintaining' rather than increasing CapEx, that's the catalyst to sell semis; (2) Up to 40% of planned data center builds are already delayed due to transformer shortages, aluminum, and permitting issues in states like Wisconsin (where states are forcing data centers to pay for their own grid upgrades); (3) Peter Boockvar expects 60-70% downside in specific semiconductor names before the cycle ends — the group is cyclical and a major bust always follows a major boom; watch price-to-sales rather than P/E at cyclical peaks; (4) Ross Hendricks: '$85 is the new $65' for oil — structural multi-year bid from strategic reserve refilling and supply chain reworking; watch the 12-36 month back-end oil futures curve for structural signals; (5) The post-GFC 'buy every dip' playbook is broken in an inflationary regime — central banks cannot cut rates or use QE to create a V-bottom like before; (6) SpaceX IPO: technologically impressive but $28T TAM is self-reported by Elon Musk — treat self-reported TAM as marketing, not data; demand audited financials.
