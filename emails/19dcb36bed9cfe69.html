Key takeaways from the roundtable: (1) AI trade is 'completely splintered' — semis showing 1999-style behavior while software collapses; if hyperscalers (Meta, Google) announce they're 'just maintaining' rather than increasing CapEx this week, it will be a catalyst to sell semis. (2) Data center construction bottleneck: up to 40% of planned builds are already delayed due to transformer/aluminum shortages and permitting issues (Wisconsin forcing data centers to pay for own grid upgrades), not chip supply. (3) One panelist expects semiconductor holdings to fall 60-70% before bust ends — use price-to-sales (not P/E) to see true risk when margins are cyclically elevated. (4) Oil floor now $85 ('85 is the new 65') even if Middle East war ends — rotate into energy/commodities on pullbacks, particularly the 12-36 month futures curve as a leading indicator. (5) Small Modular Reactors: still no licensed design in the US, illegal to build for power generation, NRC approval at 'speed of continental drift' — delay any investment despite narrative. (6) V-bottom playbook is obsolete: central banks can't cut or use QE with current inflation — do not assume quick recovery in tech drawdowns. (7) SpaceX IPO: engineering marvel but self-reported $28T TAM by CEO should be treated as marketing, not data.
