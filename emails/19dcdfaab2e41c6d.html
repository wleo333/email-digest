The Fundraising Cheat Code Most Founders Discover Too Late

From: The Founder's Corner Apr 27, 2026 Label: Investing AI Summary 
Data: 95% of cold investor outreach gets zero reply; warm introductions convert at 58%+. The early-stage round typically takes 3-6 months from first meeting to wire. Starting warm relationship-building 6 months before opening a round means you're choosing between competing term sheets rather than racing against runway. The playbook by phase — Months 1-2: Build a tiered investor list (75-100 names) filtered by stage fit, sector fit, geography, then warm intro path. Tier 1 (15-20): dream leads with existing warm intro. Send a 'permission to update' email — not a pitch, just asking to send occasional updates. Months 3-4: Send monthly investor updates under 400 words, on the first Tuesday/Wednesday of each month: one concrete headline win with numbers, 4 key metrics (MRR, users, burn rate, runway), one honest challenge, goals for next month, and optionally one specific intro ask (not vague — name the sector and company type). Founders who send consistent updates are 2x as likely to raise follow-on funding (Visible.vc data). By month 5-6, an investor who has watched you state and hit goals for 5 months is already halfway to yes when you open the round. Carta 2025 data: median seed-to-Series A interval is now 1.9 years (record high); deal counts fell 28% YoY.
