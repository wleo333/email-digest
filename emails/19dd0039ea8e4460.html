Wall Street is WRONG about Oracle

From: George Noble Apr 27, 2026 Label: Investing AI Summary 
Noble's bearish thesis on Oracle (stock down 47% from September high, currently $172): (1) Non-current debt ballooned 46% YoY to $124.7B, plus additional billions in off-balance-sheet project financing structures; total liabilities $206B vs. shareholders' equity of $39B — a 5-to-1 leverage ratio; (2) Interest expense up 32% YoY; FCF is -$24.7B trailing; $48B capex vs. ~$17B in operating cash flow; Oracle issued $43B in senior notes in 9 months; (3) Fired 30,000 employees (18% of workforce) in a single morning on March 31 — TD Cowen estimates this saves $8-10B in annual cash flow; (4) Net income up 95% last quarter, partly because Oracle extended server useful life to 6 years, reducing depreciation by billions — accounting flatters the income statement while the balance sheet deteriorates; (5) Of the $553B in Remaining Performance Obligations, ~$300B is a single contract with OpenAI through Stargate; revenue doesn't start flowing until 2027; OpenAI expects to lose $167B+ through 2028 even at $100B annual revenue; (6) Stargate's flagship Abilene campus: only 2 of 8 planned buildings operational (200MW of planned 1.2GW) after nearly 2 years; lenders redirected Abilene capacity from OpenAI to Microsoft due to credit exposure concerns; total 7GW Stargate buildout estimated at $340B; (7) GPU architectures refresh annually — hardware financed over long debt horizons becomes obsolete in 3 years.
